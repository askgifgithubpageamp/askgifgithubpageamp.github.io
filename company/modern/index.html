Didone is a genre of serif typeface that emerged in the late 18th century and was the standard style of general-purpose printing during the nineteenth. It is characterized by: Narrow and unbracketed serifs. Vertical orientation of weight axes. Strong contrast between thick and thin lines. Some stroke endings show ball terminals.
